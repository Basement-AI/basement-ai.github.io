About Me

Research and Impact

I'm a researcher in natural language processing and computational linguistics, working at the intersection of formal semantics, grammar, and AI.

Formal Foundations

I investigate formal models of grammar and semantics to understand how meaning is composed in natural language.

Machine Learning

I train, apply and evaluate statistical and neural methods—particularly large language models—on tasks that require understanding, reasoning, and generalization.

Research Philosophy

I believe in research that balances theoretical depth with practical relevance, aiming to produce insights that endure and systems that work.

Research Areas

My Focus Areas

I explore core challenges at the intersection of language, logic, and learning—combining formal and statistical approaches to build systems that understand and reason with language.

Language Understanding

Developing models that move beyond pattern matching toward deep semantic interpretation and contextual understanding.

Formal & Computational Linguistics

Exploring grammar, meaning, and compositionality through symbolic and logic-based frameworks.

Reasoning & Inference

Designing systems that perform structured reasoning, entailment, and generalization in real-world settings.

Large Language Models

Interrogating the capabilities and limitations of LLMs in tasks involving meaning, structure, and generalization.
